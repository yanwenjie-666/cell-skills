# Cell Press Manuscript Structure — Detailed Reference

## Complete Manuscript Template

### Title Page Elements

#### Title
- Maximum 120 characters (Cell); other journals may allow longer
- Informative: conveys the key finding
- No terminal punctuation
- Avoid unnecessary articles ("A" / "The") at the beginning if possible
- Gene names in italics; species names in italics

#### Author Line
- Full first name, middle initial(s), last name
- Numbered superscripts for affiliations
- Corresponding symbols for Lead Contact
- Example: "John A. Smith¹²⁵*, Mary B. Jones³⁴, Robert C. Williams¹²"

#### Affiliations
- Department, Institution, City, State/Province ZIP, Country
- Number sequentially matching author superscripts

#### Lead Contact
- Designated with asterisk (*) 
- Statement: "*Correspondence: email@university.edu"
- Alternative: "Lead Contact: email@university.edu"
- Cell Press uses "Lead Contact" NOT "Corresponding Author"

### Summary Rules (Expanded)

**Cell Press calls this "Summary" — never "Abstract"**

Length limits by manuscript type:
- Research Article: 150 words
- Resource: 150 words
- Report (Current Biology): 150 words
- Short Article (Cell Reports): 150 words

Structural formula (paragraph form, no subheadings):
```
[CONTEXT] The field/problem statement. [1-2 sentences]
[GAP] What remains unknown/unresolved. [1 sentence]
[APPROACH] "Here, we [method/approach] to [goal]." [1-2 sentences]
[RESULTS] Key findings with quantification where possible. [2-4 sentences]
[SIGNIFICANCE] Why this matters for the field. [1 sentence]
```

Strict rules:
- ❌ No citations (zero references)
- ❌ No undefined abbreviations (DNA, RNA, mRNA are acceptable)
- ❌ No figure references
- ❌ No "In this study, we..."
- ✅ Self-contained (reader needs no other context)
- ✅ Specific results (not "we found interesting results")
- ✅ Present tense for conclusions, past tense for specific results

### Highlights

Format requirements:
- Exactly 3 or 4 bullet points
- Each bullet: ≤85 characters (including spaces)
- Character count is STRICT — editors will reject over-length highlights
- No period at end of bullet
- Begin with active verb or noun phrase

Good patterns:
```
• [Noun phrase] [verb] [key finding]
• [Method/tool] reveals [discovery]
• [Finding] suggests/enables/identifies [implication]
```

Common mistakes:
- ❌ Starting with "We" (too author-centric)
- ❌ Including methods details
- ❌ Generic statements ("This study advances the field")
- ❌ Exceeding 85 characters

### eTOC Blurb

- ~50 words (40–60 acceptable range)
- Written in third person: "[Last name] et al. show that..."
- Plain language accessible to scientists outside the field
- States main finding AND significance
- No jargon or unexplained abbreviations
- ONE paragraph, 2–3 sentences typically

### Keywords

- Up to 10 keywords
- Mix specific and broad terms
- Include: technique names, biological processes, disease names, organism
- Separate with semicolons
- Example: "single-cell RNA-seq; tumor heterogeneity; drug resistance; epigenetics; BRD4; patient-derived organoids; lineage tracing"

### Introduction Best Practices

Paragraph-by-paragraph guide:
1. **Opening (broad)**: Major biological question or clinical problem
2. **Field context**: What's known; key prior discoveries (cite selectively)
3. **Knowledge gap**: What specifically remains unresolved
4. **Bridge** (optional): Why this gap matters; what makes it tractable now
5. **"Here, we..." paragraph**: Brief overview of approach and key findings

Formatting rules:
- End with: "Here, we [approach]. We demonstrate/show/find that [key results]. Our findings reveal/identify [significance]."
- Do NOT provide detailed results — just the headline findings
- Cite 20–40 references (selective, not exhaustive)
- No figure references in Introduction

### Results Organization

#### Subsection Structure
Each subsection represents one logical experiment/analysis unit:

```
## Declarative Heading in Title Case

[Motivation]: "We/To [reason/question], we..."  (1-2 sentences)
[Approach]: Brief method mention  (1 sentence)
[Data]: Results with quantification and figure references  (3-6 sentences)
[Conclusion]: What this means / transition to next section  (1-2 sentences)
```

#### Subheading Rules
- Title case capitalization
- Declarative (states conclusion)
- Contains the biological finding, not the method
- Examples:
  - "A Rare Tumor Cell Population Survives Drug Treatment"
  - "Surviving Cells Show Epigenetic Rather Than Genetic Alterations"
  - "BRD4 Inhibition Eliminates the Resistant Subpopulation"

#### Data Presentation in Text
- Quantify: "3.5-fold increase (p = 0.003, n = 5)"
- Always reference figures: "(Figure 2A)"
- Multiple figure references: "(Figures 2A and 2B)" or "(Figure 2A–2C)"
- Supplementary: "(Figure S2A)"

### Discussion Framework

| Paragraph | Content | Length |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| 1 | Key findings summary + main significance | 3-4 sentences |
| 2-3 | Mechanistic implications; how findings fit known biology | 4-6 sentences each |
| 4 | Comparison to published work; agreements and discrepancies | 4-6 sentences |
| 5 | Limitations and caveats | 3-5 sentences |
| 6 | Future directions; testable predictions | 3-5 sentences |
| 7 | Broader significance and closing statement | 2-4 sentences |

### STAR Methods Required Structure

```
STAR★METHODS

RESOURCE AVAILABILITY
  Lead contact
  Materials availability
  Data and code availability

EXPERIMENTAL MODEL AND STUDY PARTICIPANT DETAILS
  [Cell lines, animal models, human subjects, organisms]
  [Include: source, sex, age, strain, culture conditions, ethical approvals]

METHOD DETAILS
  [Organized by technique/experiment type]
  [Full protocol-level detail]
  [Catalog numbers, concentrations, incubation times]
  [Software versions and parameters]

QUANTIFICATION AND STATISTICAL ANALYSIS
  [Every test used in the paper]
  [Software used for analysis]
  [Definition of n, biological vs technical replicates]
  [Multiple comparison corrections]
  [How significance thresholds were determined]
  [Power analysis if applicable]
```

### Author Contributions (CRediT Taxonomy)

Available roles:
- Conceptualization
- Methodology
- Software
- Validation
- Formal Analysis
- Investigation
- Resources
- Data Curation
- Writing – Original Draft
- Writing – Review & Editing
- Visualization
- Supervision
- Project Administration
- Funding Acquisition

Format: "Role, A.B.C., D.E.F., and G.H.I.;"

### Declaration of Interests

Required language:
- If none: "The authors declare no competing interests."
- If present: List specific relationships for each author

### Supplemental Information

- Figures: Figure S1, S2, S3... (same format as main figures)
- Tables: Table S1, S2, S3...
- Data: Data S1 (for large datasets)
- Videos: Video S1, S2...
- All must be referenced from main text
- Include: Supplemental Figure Legends, Supplemental Table Legends

## Journal-Specific Variations

### Cell (flagship)
- No strict word limit on main text (but typically 8,000–10,000 total)
- Highest bar for novelty and significance
- Summary: 150 words exactly

### Cell Reports
- Main text: ~5,000 words
- Resource articles have different structure
- Short Articles: reduced figures (4 main + 4 supplementary)

### Current Biology
- Report format: shorter (~3,500 words main text)
- 4 main figures maximum
- Summary: 150 words
- Results and Discussion can be combined

### Immunity / Neuron
- Standard Cell Press structure
- Field-specific terminology expectations
- Typically 5,000–6,000 words main text
